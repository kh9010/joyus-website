Interactive Installation

The Line Before

A visitor approaches a touchscreen. They press CREATE. A fragment of poetry appears — a few words that breathe poetic, in a way that AI still can't do. Below it, two words: grip and release. The visitor selects. A second line, chosen by their choice. A third. A fourth.

Each time, a dichotomy — sublimate or crystallise, echo or ignite, smother or howl. When the fourth line lands, the poem takes form into a flower, its petals and colors shaped by the affinities of the fragments. The flower drifts into a garden made of every previous visitor's poem.

A poet wrote the fragments. An artist created the petals. A large language model scored each one across four emotional axes. And now the visitor makes a choice by instinct. Three kinds of intelligence, together creating meaning.

230 poetry fragments · 4 emotional axes · Exhibited at Boulder Public Library, Jan 2025 · Live prototype

About Joyus

We are Joyus — a studio of makers. We believe in storytelling, design, and imagination. We work in product design, brand building, and system design with a playful, human-centered lens. We help people connect, create, and bring forth work that feels deep, deliberate, and joyful.

We work on projects with impact. The projects it feels impossible to hire consultants for. The projects that change the lives of the people who touch them. The projects that'll haunt you until the day they're made.

Insights lie at the edge of reality and intent.

Kahran Singh

A poet and creative technologist whose work sits at the intersection of language, emotion, and systems. His poetry explores the textures of memory, desire, fear, and wonder. He is interested in what happens when we create new ways of engaging — what is poetry when it's written computationally? What are the limits of experience and environment? What happens when we blur those boundaries?

Divya Tak

A multidisciplinary creative whose work spans film, games, comics, interactive systems, and generative media, contributing to award-winning projects while pursuing a sustained inquiry into wonder, curiosity, and belonging. Trained in physics and shaped by over a decade working across creative industries, she approaches storytelling as a study of perception itself — how we see, what we overlook, and how meaning forms between systems and humans.
