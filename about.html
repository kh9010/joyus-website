Two-Person Experience Design Studio

About Joyus

We're a two-person studio that helps startups and mission-driven companies build real relationships with the people they're trying to reach. We focus on how people actually behave — not how a spreadsheet says they should — and use that to make smarter work.

Our thing is the overlap between design, behavioral science, and storytelling. Most agencies pick one. We do all three — and we've shipped work across the US, UK, India, and Hong Kong to prove it.

Good work starts with the right question — not the right answer.

Our Founders

Kahran Singh

Co-founder, Strategy

Kahran brings product design, product marketing, and strategic storytelling to Joyus. With over a decade of experience across education, travel, technology, and consumer goods — working across the US, UK, EU, and India — he's built a unique perspective on problem-solving and an ability to adapt to wildly different markets and client needs.

As an active angel investor with deep connections to both the Seattle and Indian startup ecosystems, Kahran works closely with CEOs and founders to find clarity in their messaging and build lasting relationships with the people who matter most to their business. He holds a B.Sc. in Computer Science from Tufts University.

Divya Tak

Co-founder, Design

Divya is an artist at heart, storyteller in the head, with designer hands. Her background spans experience design, product design & development, and game design. She uses the newest technology and insights into consumer behavior to craft culture-shaping experiences across industries including technology, consumer goods, and education.

Divya has a Master's degree in Physics from IIT Kharagpur and taught herself design by making a lot of things. Her illustrations — playful characters, intricate watercolors, and expressive line work — have become a signature of Joyus's creative output, from the Tatsam mental health app to Secret Senses packaging to The Line Before, an interactive poetry installation currently in development.

How We Work

We grew up at the intersection of design, product, and marketing. That means we don't hand off problems — we work them from diagnosis to delivery. We pull from behavioral economics, gamification, and a lot of varied experience to make things that actually hold up in the real world.

Every project is shaped around you — your stage, your constraints, your weird specific situation. We're just as happy doing a single focused session as a months-long partnership, and we adapt as we learn.

What Sets Us Apart

Interdisciplinary

We don't separate strategy from craft, or research from design. We pull from design thinking, behavioral economics, and gamification — and we've done it across enough industries and geographies that the cross-pollination is the whole point.

Culturally Sensitive

We've worked across India, the US, the UK, and Hong Kong. That means we borrow ideas freely — like using distribution networks for brand growth, something that's common in India but almost unheard of in the West.

Play as Practice

Joy and rigor aren't opposites. The projects that'll haunt you until the day they're made are the ones worth doing. We bring play, curiosity, and obsessive craft to everything — because the best work happens when you actually care.

Long-Term Partnerships

We don't do drive-by consulting. We stick around, check how things landed, and look for what to build next together. Our best client relationships span years.

Hands-On & Fast

We're smaller than a big firm and that's the point. We move fast, we make things ourselves, and we don't have six layers between the idea and the work.

AI-Native

We don't just talk about AI — we use it to make things. We help teams cut through the hype and build real workflows that actually save time and make better work.

The Line Before

Interactive Installation — In Development

A touchscreen installation where visitors assemble a poem by choosing between emotional poles — drawing from 230 fragments written across several years of poetry. Each choice shapes a generative flower; every flower joins a living garden of all previous visitors' poems. Submitted to Science Gallery Bengaluru's INTELLIGENCE open call.
