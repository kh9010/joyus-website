Five concepts for services.
Two of those, extended to work.

Each concept is a different answer to the same question: what should this page look like when a bullet list is the worst option? Same palette and type across all seven so you're comparing structure, not swatches.

services page — 5 directions
01 · the ledger
A log of every engagement, tagged and dated.
Proof-over-claim. Refuses the services page entirely. Filter by advise / design / build / tell / grow.
02 · pillars & process
Three pillars on top, a 5-step process below.
Clearest readout in 30 seconds. Same capabilities shown as inventory and as flow.
03 · the verb index
Six verbs from the deck. Six sections. Six case studies.
Design, build, manage, market, advise, grow — numbered, photo-backed, scroll-snapped.
also has a work version ↓
04 · the manifesto
Six principles. No bullets. Just prose.
Most “Kahran and Divya writing” version. Voice is the differentiator.
05 · the sticker board
A toolbox of hats, scattered and pickable.
Most memorable. Most technically ambitious. Playful on top, cerebral underneath.
also has a work version ↓
work page — 2 directions (concepts 03 and 05)
03 · the gallery parade — for work
Ten full-bleed bands. Each its own poster.
The imagery IS the interface. Scroll through ten project bands — each a unique composition, not a templated card. Click “open the story” on any one and the narrative slides in as a side panel over the image. Studio X’s accordion-over-imagery, done for case work.
05 · the pinboard — for work
The studio wall, scattered. Artifacts, not labels.
A deck slide, a stat badge, a torn-paper quote, a phone mock, a swatch, an origami mark, an onboarding strip, a glitch mark, a polaroid, a sticky note. Each artifact is from a real project. Hover lifts, click scrolls to the story. Feels like walking into the studio.

The work mockups translate the style's soul into work-native structure — not the services layout transplanted.

home page — same 2 styles, home-native
X · gallery home
One tasteful opening. Two careful peeks.
Hero band with the intent box as the slab, a composed side element (tape, stacked paper, Caveat annotation). Below: one featured case as a full poster, one paired feed band (thinking + podcast). Restrained on purpose — Studio X says one great image beats ten okay ones.
Y · pinboard home
The whole home is the studio wall.
Intent box lives on a yellow sticky note — alongside a founders' polaroid (→ about), a deck slide (→ work), a podcast ticket stub (→ latest ep), a torn essay quote (→ thinking), a color swatch, a comic panel, a business card. Mouse drifts them. Every artifact is a way in.

Home X asks: “what's the one thing to show first?” Home Y asks: “what's everything to show at once?”

podcast page — same 2 styles, podcast-native
X · The Joyus Letters
The podcast, treated as a quarterly journal.
Masthead, lead feature, currently-running threads, five other pieces in the issue, curated archive cuts, editors' note. The reader opts in because the presentation is the show's sensibility — not because a band shouted “why listen.” Every episode is a piece in an issue; every quarter is a volume. You browse it like reading, not like shopping.
Y · pinboard podcast
A wall of episode artifacts. Pick one to put on.
Twelve artifacts scattered — cassette tapes, ticket stubs, napkins with scribbled quotes, guest polaroids, a voicemail slip, a matchbook for the success series, a vinyl disc, sticky notes. Each represents an episode. Mouse drifts them; click opens the player. Archive below for completionists.

Podcast X asks: “which moment should stop them?” Podcast Y asks: “what should they pick up?”
