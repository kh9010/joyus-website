the lead · Vol. III No. 03
on selling a thing nobody knows how to buy yet

Every retailer came into the room thinking they already knew what it was.

A product that worked. A pitch that didn't. And four months of un-teaching the closest thing they'd seen before.

Ep 503 · 52 min · with Dina Holzapfel, founder of Smiqql · hosted by Kahran

I kept thinking the pitch was the product. I'd walk into a meeting with a deck and a sample, and the buyer would nod in all the right places, and then a week later they'd come back with a question that made it clear they'd heard an entirely different product than the one I'd described.

Eventually I realized it wasn't about the pitch. They weren't deciding whether my product was good. They were deciding whether the product they'd already pattern-matched me to was good. And the product they'd pattern-matched me to wasn't mine.

So the whole next four months was about un-teaching. Not explaining what we were — explaining what we weren't, enough times in enough different ways that the match finally broke. That was the work. The pitch came after.

a note from the editors

This week we rerecorded part of an episode we'd already released — because the question we'd asked in it had stopped being ours.

Three months ago we put out an ep about building for a market that doesn't exist yet. We got quite a lot of mail about it, some of it kind, most of it asking a version of the same question: okay, but how do you pay rent while you're un-teaching the buyer?

We'd asked the wrong question. The one that matters isn't about existence, it's about patience. So we rerecorded it. The corrected version's in the feed now, under the same episode number. The old version's still there too, because we don't want to pretend we didn't ask it the first way. Both are true of a moment.

Kahran & Divya 17 April 2026
