XTDB

Full rebrand for a $10M ARR database company — from CRUX to XTDB

Client XTDB (formerly CRUX)
Location UK
Stage $10M ARR
Services Rebranding, Brand Guidelines
CRUX→XTDB Full rebrand completed
$10M ARR at time of rebrand
Complete Brand guidelines system

The Challenge

CRUX was a technically exceptional database — bitemporality and immutable data done right, for developers building applications where auditability and time-travel queries matter. But the name CRUX, while evocative, wasn't scaling with the company's ambition. At $10M ARR with a growing enterprise sales motion, they needed a brand that could stand up in Fortune 500 conversations and signal technical credibility without alienating the developer community that loved them.

Our Approach

The name XTDB preserved the technical DNA — XT for extended/cross-temporal, DB for database, with the double-letter pattern creating a distinctive visual mark that worked in code, in conversation, and in competitive decks. We built the brand system from the inside out: starting with what made the technology genuinely novel, and building a visual language that made that novelty tangible.

The brand guidelines we developed gave the XTDB team clear rules for how to use the identity across contexts — from developer documentation to sales collateral to conference booths. The mark was designed to work at every scale, from favicon to billboard.

The Outcome

XTDB launched its new identity at a key industry moment, with a brand system robust enough for enterprise sales and distinctive enough for developer love. The guidelines gave the team the clarity they needed to execute consistently without a full in-house design team. The rebrand coincided with a significant phase of enterprise customer growth.
