PrathamUSA

Rebuilding the donor journey for a $50M nonprofit

Client PrathamUSA
Location USA
Organization Type $50M Nonprofit
Services Donor Research, Process Creation, Information Flow
$50M Annual fundraising at stake
Redesigned End-to-end donor journey
Research-led Insights-driven transformation

The Challenge

PrathamUSA raises $50M annually to support education access across India. Their donor experience had grown organically over years — meaning it had also grown fragmented, inconsistent, and difficult to navigate. Donors were falling through gaps. Information wasn't flowing where it needed to go. The organization's remarkable impact wasn't being communicated in a way that compelled continued giving.

Our Approach

We began with deep donor research — interviews across segments, from first-time givers to long-term major donors. The insights were clear and painful: donors felt underinformed, sometimes forgotten, often unsure whether their contribution had mattered. They wanted connection to impact, not just receipts and annual reports.

We designed a new information architecture for the donor journey: what communication happens when, who sends it, what story it tells. We created process documentation that the team could actually use — not 60-page PDF decks, but clear flowcharts, templates, and triggers. The goal was making the invisible visible, so the team could execute consistently at scale.

The Outcome

PrathamUSA now has a donor journey that matches the quality of the work they fund. Donors receive communications that connect their gift to real children, real schools, real outcomes. The process changes gave the team clarity on who owns what and when — reducing the operational friction that had been silently eroding relationships for years.
