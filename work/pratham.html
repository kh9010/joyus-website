PrathamUSA

Donor Research, Process Creation, Information Flow

Client PrathamUSA
Location USA
Size 50-100 Employees, $50M Nonprofit
Services Donor Research, Process Creation, Information Flow
$50M nonprofit organization
6 stages in donor journey model
$2K–$10K target donor segment at risk

Challenge

PrathamUSA, a large nonprofit, saw a catastrophic drop in donations in 2020 and into 2021. Fundraising galas were eviscerated and many of the $2,000-$10,000 donors disappeared with them. These donors were the feeder source for Pratham's super committed $10,000+ donors, and losing them would hurt the organization for years to come.

Approach

Joyus engaged with Pratham to determine what brought donors in at different levels, what caused them to increase their donations, and what could recreate the donor journey post-2020. We created a 6-stage donor journey model (from Unaware to Permanent Bond) through semi-structured interviews with staff and donors. We mapped the information flow to identify where breakdowns were occurring — discovering that information filtered through chapter leaders was creating inconsistencies. We found that high-value, poorly scalable experiences (like site visits to schools in India) were key to forming the highest-value relationships, challenging the assumption that digital-first was always better.

Outcome

The donor journey framework gave Pratham a shared language for discussing donors beyond annual giving levels. The information flow mapping revealed specific breakdowns that could be addressed operationally. The insight that long-form publications (previously considered low-value due to low view counts) were actually correlated with higher-value donors shifted strategy from cutting costs to investing more strategically.
