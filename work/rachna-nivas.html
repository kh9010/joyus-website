a case study — rachna nivas

One brand book. Many hands.

Rachna Nivas is a NYC-based Kathak artist, choreographer and educator — trained under Pandit Chitresh Das, co-founder of Leela Dance Collective. She came to us while applying for fellowships and residencies, with a voice that was getting lost in the weight of a tradition she embodied.

We built her a brand book. Then we stepped out of the room. What follows is the work other people do on top of it — without us, and mostly without her.

Who is Rachna?

Rachna Nivas is a Kathak dancer, choreographer, and educator based in New York City. Trained under Pandit Chitresh Das and a co-founder of Leela Dance Collective, she has spent decades carrying a classical tradition — not preserving it behind glass, but pressing it into the present. Her practice is simultaneously a discipline, a philosophy, and a form of activism. She teaches, she performs, she builds institutions. The brand book begins here — with Rachna's own words, in her own voice, on red — because every collaborator who picks it up needs to start from who she is, not from a summary of what she does.

the voice page. written first-person, on red, so every collaborator starts from Rachna's own words — not a summary of them.

how to write, frame, and direct the body — a sacred instrument and an athletic engine, never a pose.
