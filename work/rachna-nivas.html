a case study — rachna nivas

Standing out as a classical dancer.

Rachna Nivas, a NYC-based artist, choreographer and classical dancer, came to us looking to create space for more dynamism in her profile — as an educator, activist, and preserver of the classical tradition and dance form of Kathak. Rachna wanted to own her own distinct identity, while remaining true to the legacy of the 5,000-year-old art form.

Joyus worked with Rachna to create an identity that was true to her — and to build a process to keep it feeling fresh without occupying her time and energy.

first — find what only she can do

When you are already good, leverage what you are good at.

Rachna engaged with Joyus when she needed a new way of talking about herself, as she applied for new fellowships and residencies. After analyzing her website and applications, we realized her voice was getting obscured — lost in the weight of the tradition she embodied.

From years as an activist, in the classroom, and excelling in a storytelling art form, Rachna has a rare ability to carry an audience with her. She didn't need help writing better copy — she needed help bringing the skills she had honed in person to the written form.

↓ then — break the rules, on purpose

following the strong signal — not the loudest one

Authenticity lets you break the rules.

Rachna, like many dependent on reputation, constantly finds herself bombarded with helpful tips on how to grow her reach. While most would encourage her to make frequent, short, digestible videos — especially given the lack of audience familiarity with her art form — we encouraged her to follow what felt authentic.

These authenticity-forward posts, despite breaking the rules around optimal length or simplifying for the audience, have delivered for Rachna — many times more social engagements, and, perhaps more crucially, a rising presence in the New York City arts scene.

↓ then — own the identity, even when it grows

a brand guide that travels

Create the identity and own it — even when it grows big.

As we worked with Rachna, she realized she needed her own brand identity. Working with her guru sisters had launched her career — but now she needed an identity of her own. We translated this into a visual guide and brand book, enabling Rachna as she finalized her decision to build a new brand for her teaching persona and school.
