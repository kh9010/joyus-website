Challenge

Rachna Nivas, a NYC-based performing artist, choreographer, and classical Kathak dancer, needed to create space for more dynamism in her professional profile. As an educator, activist, and preserver of the 5000+ year old dance form of Kathak, Rachna wanted to own her distinct identity while remaining true to the legacy of the art form. She was applying for new fellowships and residencies, but her powerful voice was getting obscured by the weight of tradition. How could she show why she belonged without making a defensive case?

Approach

Working closely with Rachna to uncover her motivations, values, and unique perspective, we helped her communicate why she belonged in prestigious spaces rather than making a formal case for it. We translated her powerful in-person presence — her depth of knowledge, her passion, her clarity — into written form that could live on the page and screen.

Rather than encouraging frequent, short, digestible social media content following conventional wisdom, we encouraged Rachna to follow what felt authentic. This meant longer, deeper posts that leaned into her strong base of knowledge about Kathak history, technique, and cultural significance. We created a visual guide and brand book covering her voice, imagery, aesthetic preferences, and content approach — giving her team clear direction for supporting her evolving presence.

Outcome

These authenticity-forward posts, despite breaking social media "rules" about optimal length and frequency, delivered many times more social engagement than shorter content would have. More importantly, Rachna gained a rising presence in the New York City arts scene. By refusing to dilute her voice for algorithmic preference, she attracted meaningful connections with curators, collaborators, and institutions who valued depth and substance. More authenticity led to more meaningful contacts and more meaningful collaborations — fellowships, residencies, and speaking opportunities. The brand identity became less about marketing and more about creating a coherent presence that honored both the tradition she preserves and the innovation she brings.
