Challenge

Agemo needed to create an AI-powered app that would let users build backend workflows and draft productivity tools in minutes without requiring engineers. The fundamental challenge was making an inherently complex AI tool feel approachable while giving users confidence in the output. How do you make the impossible feel possible?

Approach

Our research phase began with extensive user interviews, uncovering a critical insight: users didn't want just generation — they wanted the ability to iterate and refine. The initial AI output was a starting point, not an endpoint. This discovery shaped everything.

We designed a visual grammar that reinforced space, flexibility, and play rather than technical complexity. The chat interface coaches users through initial creation, with a UX that supports refinement at every step. We added a browsable library of community-built functions to show the product's tangible value and give users more starting points for adapting use cases to their needs. The interface celebrates iteration as a feature, not a limitation.

Outcome

An interface that makes AI development approachable through a refinement-first paradigm, community examples, and a visual language that communicates play and possibility rather than technical complexity. Users can see themselves in the product — not as engineers, but as builders. The community library became a powerful growth lever, with successful user-created functions inspiring others to try. The thoughtful interface design removed friction from the AI workflow, making the tool feel less like complex technology and more like a creative partner.
