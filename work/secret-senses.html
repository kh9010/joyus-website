Secret Senses

From sketchbook to shelf: hand-drawn watercolors as product packaging

Client Secret Senses
Category Consumer Goods
Services Identity Design, Illustrations, Packaging
Hand-drawn Original watercolor illustrations
Shelf-ready From concept to packaging
Identity Complete brand system

The Challenge

Secret Senses came to Joyus with beautiful watercolor illustrations and a dream of turning them into a real product brand. The challenge: how do you take something inherently personal and handmade — the texture of watercolor on paper, the imperfection that makes hand-drawn art human — and bring it into the world as commercial packaging without losing the soul?

Our Approach

Divya's illustration work was the heart of this project. She developed a series of watercolor botanicals that captured the sensory world Secret Senses wanted to inhabit — lush, intimate, alive with quiet detail. The design challenge was in the translation: how to reproduce hand-drawn work at print scale while preserving the warmth that makes it compelling.

We built the brand identity system around the illustrations rather than alongside them. Typography, color palette, label layout — all were designed to frame and elevate the artwork rather than compete with it. The result is packaging that looks genuinely handcrafted, because in the most important way, it is.

The Outcome

Secret Senses launched with a shelf presence that stood out in a crowded category. The hand-drawn quality became the product's most powerful differentiator — in a world of algorithmically optimized packaging design, something that felt human resonated. The brand identity system we created gives the company a foundation to extend across new products without losing coherence.
