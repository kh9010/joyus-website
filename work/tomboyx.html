The Challenge

TomboyX, a gender-neutral underwear brand, needed to raise $5M in a down market. They had incredible product metrics but needed a story that would move investors — one that demonstrated not just traction but a fundamentally better business model.

Our Approach

Joyus worked with TomboyX to distill their operational advantages into a narrative that spoke investors' language. Rather than leading with identity politics, we led with unit economics: 72% product margins against a 53% market average, a 2.5% return rate versus 30% industry standard, and 50% repeat customer rates. We positioned these as evidence of product-market love, not just fit.

The Outcome

The investor deck told a story of operational excellence wrapped in cultural relevance. TomboyX raised the full $5M, with investors citing the clarity of the business case. The brand went on to achieve $25M in revenue with 55,000+ five-star reviews, proving that the story we told was grounded in reality.
