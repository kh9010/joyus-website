TomboyX

Investor storytelling that raised $5M in a challenging market

Client TomboyX
Location USA
Company Type DTC Fashion, 20–50 Employees
Services Brand Story, Investor Pitch
$5M Raised in a down market
72% Product margins (vs 53% market avg)
2.5% Return rate (vs 30% industry)
76 NPS score

The Challenge

TomboyX, a gender-neutral underwear brand, came to Joyus needing to raise $5M in a down market. They had built something genuinely exceptional — superior product margins, return rates that blew away industry averages, and a fiercely loyal customer base. But their story wasn't landing. Investors were hearing about identity and culture when they needed to hear about unit economics and defensibility.

Our Approach

We reframed everything around the metrics. A 72% gross margin against the 53% industry average isn't a brand story — it's proof of operational excellence. A 2.5% return rate against 30% industry standard means customers love what they buy. An NPS of 76 in a commodity category means brand love. We built a narrative that led with numbers and let the culture story follow as proof, not premise.

The pitch became about the structural advantages of building a brand with this level of product truth: when customers return 12x less than average, when margins let you reinvest in community rather than discounts, when NPS drives word-of-mouth at scale — that's a compounding flywheel. We made that flywheel visible.

The Outcome

TomboyX raised the full $5M. Investors cited the clarity of the business case. The brand went on to achieve $25M in annual revenue with 55,000+ five-star reviews — proving the story we helped tell was grounded in reality, not aspiration. Sometimes the best brand strategy is simply making the truth undeniable.
