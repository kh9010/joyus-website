tomboyx

Beloved underwear company in need of a refreshed story to raise a re-capitalization in a down round.

  • Three decks
  • Three audiences
United States 10–20 employees $25M annual revenue

Three audiences, three angles, same Tomboy. Here's how we told it for operators, for values investors, and for syndicates.

beat 01 — how we opened

The first slide.

for the operator

Led with unit economics on slide one. Operators decide in 90 seconds whether the math is real — we gave them the math first, narrative second.

for the values investor

Led with the community — who buys tomboyx, who shows up at events, who writes in. Values investors want proof their people are here.

for the syndicate

Led with the founder on camera, one slide, no bullets. Syndicates package stories for LPs — the LPs need to remember one thing.

beat 02 — how we talked market

How we framed the market.

for the operator

Highlighted strong metrics in a way accessible for investors unfamiliar with the underwear sector. Market forces drove tomboyx to the breaking point — not leadership decisions that early investors might contextualize differently.

for the values investor

Language called to investors' values, framing the lack of traditional VC funding for women and queer founders as the market opportunity — not the problem.

for the syndicate

Quantified market growth and the outcomes of LGBTQ consumer shifts. Language captured the exceptional metrics while keeping warmth at the forefront — syndicates need to re-tell this to their LPs.

beat 03 — how we brought in the team

How we brought in the team.

for the operator

Guided investors through typical diligence questions with a mix of data and narrative. Operators want to know who runs the business — the deck put the team on slide three, not slide fifteen.

for the values investor

Reinforced the lived truth of the brand — tomboyx supports XXS–XXXXXL, with diverse silhouettes reflected in every team and product decision.

for the syndicate

Layout drew minimal attention to individual histories — focus stayed on the team as a unit. Syndicates pitch to LPs who'll hear the story second-hand; the team slide has to survive the re-telling.

beat 04 — the repudiation

The Victoria's Secret beat.

for the operator

Clear, simple language and visuals supported the intense brand ethos. For operators, the point was market positioning: tomboyx isn't trying to beat Victoria's Secret. It owns the market Victoria's Secret abandoned.

for the values investor

Imagery nodded to the female gaze in a deliberate repudiation of male-gaze focused marketing. For values investors, "not Victoria's Secret" isn't a market gap — it's a moral position.

for the syndicate

Showcased recent wedding and graduation invitations from fans — artifacts of the emotional resonance investors find with the deck. Syndicates need a story their LPs can repeat at dinner; this was it.
