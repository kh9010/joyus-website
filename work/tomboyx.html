beat 01 — how we opened

The first slide.

for the operator

Opened with metrics framed so an investor new to the underwear sector could read them. The deck's first move was proof the math is sound.

for the values investor

Opened on the funding story, not the cap table. The first frame argued that traditional VC has underfunded women and queer founders — and that's the opportunity, not the problem.

for the syndicate

Opened warm. Exceptional metrics were there, but the language led with the people and the feeling — so the LPs hearing this second-hand would remember it.

beat 02 — how we talked market

How we framed the market.

for the operator

Quantified market growth and the outcomes of LGBTQ consumer shifts. Framed the breakdown as market forces, not leadership decisions — because early operators will contextualize decisions differently than new angels.

for the values investor

Spoke to the lack of traditional VC funding for women and queer founders — framed as the opening, not the wound. The market is the undercapitalized majority, and Tomboy is already in it.

for the syndicate

Exceptional metrics, warm language. Syndicates needed numbers their LPs would believe and a tone they'd repeat. No chart without a sentence next to it.

beat 03 — how we brought in the team

How we brought in the team.

for the operator

Guided angels through typical diligence questions with a mix of data and narrative. Team slide came early — operators want to know who's running it before they ask anything else.

for the values investor

Reinforced the lived truth of the brand — Tomboy supports XXS–XXXXXL, and the team looks like the customer. Not accidentally.

for the syndicate

Layout drew attention to the team as a unit, not individual histories. Syndicates pitch second-hand — the slide had to survive the re-telling without naming the wrong person.

beat 04 — how we closed

How each deck closed.

for the operator

Closed with a data tour that pre-empted the diligence questions angels were about to ask. Detailed enough to answer; short enough to forward.

for the values investor

Closed on the customer community. Imagery nodded to the female gaze — a deliberate repudiation of male-gaze-focused marketing. The close wasn't a number. It was a portrait. Every body Victoria's Secret called wrong — that's the Tomboy customer.

for the syndicate

Closed with fan artifacts — wedding and graduation invitations the company has received from customers. The moat isn't in the P&L. It's in the mailbox.
