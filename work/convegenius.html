ConveGenius

User Research, Gamification, Motivation and Behavioral Design

Client ConveGenius
Location India
Size 300+ Employees, 75 Cr INR Revenue
Services User Research, Gamification, Behavioral Design
100K monthly active students
Growth + Engagement dual framework for success metrics
6 student motivation types identified

Challenge

ConveGenius, an education-technology company delivering value to schools and state governments in India, had 100,000 monthly active students but found they treated the app like homework — only doing what was mandatory. ConveGenius wanted to engage students more deeply and help them find learning to be fun. They came to Joyus with the idea of adding a rewards layer.

Approach

Rather than treating gamification as a superficial add-on, we reframed it as a behavior change problem. We helped ConveGenius define success around two goals: growth (acquisition of students for under-used features) and engagement (bringing students back to features they'd previously discovered). We identified 6 student motivation types and mapped them to different reward categories, creating a system where rewards could be appropriately targeted. We designed wireframes and user journey maps showing how the reward system would integrate with existing screens, and created an effort-vs-frequency matrix to ensure each reward type balanced difficulty with payoff.

Outcome

The growth-vs-engagement framework gave ConveGenius clarity on how to measure feature success — preventing features designed for long-term engagement from being judged by short-term growth metrics. The motivation-reward matching system provided a principled approach to feature prioritization, and the wireframes enabled a clean handoff to development.
