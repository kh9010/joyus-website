a case study — convegenius

Gamification looks like an obvious fix for engagement. How do you turn it into a strategy?

ConveGenius is an Indian ed-tech company serving private schools and state governments. 100,000 monthly active students, a deep product across subjects and verticals.

But students treated the app like homework — only doing what was mandatory. The team wanted deeper engagement, and came to us with the idea of a rewards layer.

We broke rewards down into achievable segments, and shaped a structure their team could build. We've worked inside Indian ed-tech before — so instead of ramping up, we went straight to the work.

first — define what success means

Gamification is superficial — retention is a behaviour-change problem.

Success here meant two goals: growth — pulling students into under-used features — and engagement, bringing them back to the ones they'd already found.

The split made measurement honest. Without it, features get judged on easy metrics — views, clicks — even when they're built for something bigger.

Growth vs Engagement activities

Activities designed to bring new users into the app or expose existing users to features they haven't tried — broadly targeted, with relatively low execution cost.
Traits Familiarise the user with the app. First-time usage gives a shiny reward. Prompts students to talk about it outside the product.
Transient, low-stakes rewards (e.g. a sticker or badge visible on a profile for a limited time) that surface the product socially and prompt word-of-mouth rather than building long-term habits.
· social rewards
Activities aimed at deepening the habits of users who are already active — targeted to specific student personas, higher investment to execute well, longer payoff window.
Traits Focused on one student's learning arc. The longer you do it, the more rewarding it gets. Requires investment, and is tuned to specific personas.
Persistent, accumulating items — badges, avatars, status symbols — that students display in the app. Unlike ephemeral rewards, collectibles stay and function as social signals within the community.
·
Rewards tied to sustained effort over time — Expert Mentor Status, workshop access, title progression — that signal the system respects the user's long-term investment.
