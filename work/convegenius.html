ConveGenius

Gamification strategy for 100K monthly active students

Client ConveGenius
Location India
Category EdTech
Services User Research, Gamification, Behavioral Design
100K Monthly active students
Gamification Behavior change through play
Research-driven Insights from real classrooms

The Challenge

ConveGenius reaches 100,000 students monthly across India with AI-powered learning tools. The product worked — students who used it learned. But retention was the problem. Getting students to come back, to build habits, to treat learning as something they wanted rather than something they had to do. That's a behavioral design challenge, not a features challenge.

Our Approach

We started in classrooms. Real ones, in real schools, watching how students actually interacted with learning technology — what caught their attention, what made them lean in, what made them click away. The research revealed a consistent pattern: students responded to progress they could see and show to others. Invisible progress was no progress.

The gamification framework we built was designed around those insights. Points and badges as typically implemented don't work — they feel arbitrary. We designed achievement structures that tied directly to real learning milestones, social systems that let students share progress with peers (not parents), and streak mechanics calibrated to the actual rhythms of school days. The behavioral design model gave the product team a consistent framework to evaluate future features against.

The Outcome

The gamification strategy gave ConveGenius a coherent behavioral model to build against — one grounded in how their specific students actually behaved, not how gamification theory says students should behave. The research findings also surfaced several product improvements beyond gamification that the team hadn't previously identified.
