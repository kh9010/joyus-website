nine hats, scattered — click one

advise

Think with you, not for you.

The cheapest, most used thing we sell. An hour, a week, a quarter — time in your room, asking the questions the team has stopped asking each other. Sometimes it turns into design work. Often it doesn't.

We've done this for pre-seed founders, $50M nonprofits, Series B execs, and two-person co-founder pairs in week four of a split.

typical shape

2-week diagnostic → written POV → open-ended retainer.

billed as

Flat week rates. No time-sheets.

brand & identity

The visible shape of a decision you've already made.

Full identity systems — mark, type, color, voice, motion — built to survive the company outgrowing its first self. We like the ones where the constraints are genuinely weird (a database, a comedy startup, a Kathak dancer).

XTDB (temporal database → origami logo). Gliitch (restaurant tech → two-color glitch). Tatsam (mental health → calm + agency). Secret Senses (hair care → Procreate florals).

typical shape

3–6 weeks. Strategy first, then marks, then system.

delivers

Brand book, marks, type, usage system, design tokens.

research

Before we make, we talk to the people.

User interviews, donor interviews, customer journey work, competitive reads. Usually 8–15 conversations, synthesized into one short document you can hand to the team. We're allergic to 40-slide research readouts nobody reads.

Pratham's six-stage donor model came out of a year of quiet conversations. Agemo's onboarding came out of one weekend of watching users open the tool cold.

typical shape

2–4 weeks. Interviews + synthesis + one decision doc.

pairs well with

Advise, product, GTM.

product & interface

Figma, in close partnership with your engineers.

Product-sized UX — onboarding, core loops, new features — designed to be built. We write the spec, we sit in the PR reviews, we argue about spacing with your engineers. No designer-over-the-wall work.

Agemo's GenAI coding tool onboarding. Tatsam's full app. ConveGenius's rewards system.

typical shape

6–12 weeks. Design + eng partnership.

delivers

Flows, components, tokens, specs your team can actually build from.

behavioral design

Why people do the thing — or don't.

Grounded in behavioral science, not in lifted Duolingo patterns. We help teams tell the difference between what gets users to repeat (engagement) and what gets them to invite others (growth) — and pick the one their product actually needs.

ConveGenius picked engagement. Agemo changed the onboarding to match how exploratory users actually move.

typical shape

2–4 weeks of framework + audit, then 6-week pilot.

delivers

A framework, a set of bets, a measurement plan.

illustration

Drawing, not stock.

Hand-drawn illustration systems — packaging, editorial, product moments, comics. Divya's primary craft. We lean into this when a brand has to feel warm, personal, and defiant of templates.

Secret Senses watercolors, the entire JoyusComics catalog, editorial accents across most client work.

typical shape

Project-based. 1–8 weeks depending on scope.

medium

Procreate, print, digital motion.

go-to-market

The sprint where strategy meets shipping.

Launch sprints for zero-to-one products and new-market entries. Typically 6–10 weeks. We own positioning, creative, and the first four weeks of launch — not the ten-week distribution campaign after that.

Klydo: zero to 150 daily sales by Jan 1. Several unnamed YC companies with unusual launch constraints.

typical shape

6–10 weeks. Positioning → creative → launch → 4-week tune.

pairs with

Brand, pitch, product.

pitch & narrative

The deck isn't the deck. The story is.

Investor decks, founder origin stories, keynote narratives, internal myth-making. We interview the team, find the through-line, and write the version that holds up under the skeptical question.

TomboyX: three audiences, three decks. A down-round became a $5M raise. Countless unnamed seed decks.

typical shape

2–4 weeks. Interviews + narrative + deck design.

delivers

One deck. Sometimes three. Always with the story doc underneath.

founder coaching

The person behind the company.

Ongoing 1:1 work with founders on the things that aren't deliverable — decisions, anxieties, co-founder tension, self-presentation. Usually 60-minute sessions, biweekly. Sometimes the most important thing we do for a company.

typical shape

Ongoing. Minimum 3-month commitment.

boundaries

We're not therapists. For what coaching does and doesn't do — ask first.
